# PlayBits - Interactive Educational Games for Nepal

## Platform Overview
PlayBits is a comprehensive, interactive gaming platform designed specifically for the Nepali market and global audience interested in South Asian economics. The platform combines entertainment with financial literacy education through gamified experiences centered on real Nepali market data and everyday life scenarios.

Website: https://www.playbits.online/
Developer: Ctrl Bits
Language: English
Platform: Web-based, responsive design
Technology: Next.js, React, TypeScript, TailwindCSS

## Mission & Value Proposition
PlayBits empowers users to develop practical financial knowledge through engaging games that reflect authentic Nepali market conditions. Our mission is to make financial literacy accessible, entertaining, and culturally relevant for both Nepali citizens and international audiences interested in South Asian markets.

## Core Games & Detailed Descriptions

### 1. Guess the Price
**Purpose:** Develop price estimation skills and market awareness
**Mechanics:** Players view everyday Nepali items and predict their actual market prices
**Difficulty Levels:** 5 progressive levels from very easy to very hard
**Content:** 35+ items covering groceries, food, transport, services, health, and home categories
**Educational Value:** 
- Market awareness and price trends
- Understanding purchasing power
- Recognition of pricing patterns
- Decision-making based on incomplete information
**Items Include:** Rice, chicken meat, eggs, tomatoes, mustard oil, milk, taxi fares, electricity, gas cylinders, and more
**Scoring:** Points awarded based on accuracy (0-1000 per round)

### 2. Budget Master
**Purpose:** Develop household budget allocation and financial planning skills
**Mechanics:** Players allocate a monthly budget across essential spending categories
**Scenarios:** 3 realistic Nepali family situations
  - Middle-class family in Kathmandu (NPR 75,000/month)
  - Young professional in Pokhara (NPR 45,000/month)
  - University student in Kathmandu (NPR 25,000/month)
**Categories:** Housing, food, transport, education, healthcare, entertainment, savings
**Educational Value:**
- Budget allocation and planning
- Understanding spending categories
- Trade-off decision making
- Financial constraint management
- Recommended vs. actual spending analysis
**Feedback:** Personalized feedback on allocation quality and category-specific suggestions

### 3. Market Trends
**Purpose:** Develop predictive analysis and trend recognition skills
**Mechanics:** Players analyze 6-month price history and predict next month's price
**Items:** 6 different products with varying trends
  - Rice (Premium) - Increasing trend
  - Tomatoes - Volatile trend
  - Chicken Breast - Stable trend
  - Diesel Fuel - Increasing trend
  - Bananas - Decreasing trend
  - Milk Powder - Increasing trend
**Confidence Levels:** Low, medium, high (affects scoring multiplier)
**Educational Value:**
- Pattern recognition
- Data analysis and interpretation
- Trend forecasting
- Confidence assessment
- Risk evaluation
**Scoring:** Based on accuracy with confidence multiplier and trend bonus

### 4. Shopping Challenge
**Purpose:** Develop smart shopping decisions and budget optimization skills
**Mechanics:** Complete shopping missions within a budget while prioritizing essential items
**Challenges:** 4 different real-life shopping scenarios
  - Diwali Festival Shopping (NPR 8,000 budget, family of 4)
  - Student Monthly Expenses (NPR 4,500 budget, individual)
  - Office Worker's Monthly Shop (NPR 6,500 budget, individual)
  - Senior Citizen Savings (NPR 3,500 budget, individual)
**Item Categories:** Essentials, important, and optional priorities
**Educational Value:**
- Smart shopping decision-making
- Budget constraint satisfaction
- Priority assessment
- Trade-off optimization
- Efficiency evaluation
**Scoring:** Efficiency rating (0-100%), budget adherence, essential items coverage

### 5. Price Memory
**Purpose:** Develop memory and price association skills
**Mechanics:** Classic memory matching game with price-item pairs
**Difficulty Levels:** 3 levels
  - Easy: 8 cards (4 pairs), 2 minutes
  - Medium: 12 cards (6 pairs), 3 minutes
  - Hard: 16 cards (8 pairs), 4 minutes
**Features:** Timer, move counter, score calculation
**Educational Value:**
- Memory improvement
- Price-item association learning
- Time management
- Spatial reasoning
**Scoring:** Base score minus move penalties and time penalties plus difficulty bonuses

- **Poultry Association Records**: Official livestock market data
- **Local Market Surveys**: Community-based price collection
- **Reddit & User Reports**: Real-time crowd-sourced pricing
- **Supermarket & Retail Data**: Current retail pricing
- **Fuel Station Reports**: Energy commodity pricing
